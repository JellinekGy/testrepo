Works

Title Date Description
1937 The Hobbit is the first entry within Tolkien's fictional universe and follows the quest of home-loving Bilbo Baggins, the titular hobbit, to win a share of the treasure guarded by Smaug the dragon. Bilbo's journey takes him through the continent of Middle-Earth from his light-hearted, rural surroundings into more sinister territory.
1954 - 1955 Set in the same universe as the previous book, and Tolkien's most famous work to date. The Lord of the Rings is a sweeping, epic fantasy, following a fellowship of Hobbits, Men, Dwarves and Elves on a desperate journey to stop the story's main antagonist, the Dark Lord Sauron and his Rings of Power, that threaten to destroy all of Middle-earth.
1977 Published posthumously, The Silmarillion is a prequel to the other Middle-Earth stories, explaining the rich in-universe mythology and the origins of the species created by Tolkien.
